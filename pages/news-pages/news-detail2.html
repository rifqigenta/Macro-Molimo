4 Tips for Mixing Patterns and Textures According to Designers

29 Nov 23, by Siti Nur

For interior designers, combining patterns and textures when decorating a home is an easy task. They have the skills and insights to make the fusion of these two decorative elements harmonious and seamless.

However, there's no need to worry because it turns out that combining patterns and textures when decorating a home can be easily learned. Here are some tips on how to blend patterns and textures according to designers that you can understand.

Pay attention to the scale and materials used.

When combining various textures and patterns in your home, it is important to pay attention to scale and materials used. Christine Zippert, designer and founder of CC Zippert, provides an example that the combination of two floral patterns will be more harmonious if one pattern is larger in scale than the other.

Consider the weight of the material.

In addition to considering the scale and material, the weight of the material is also important to take into account when you want to blend patterns and textures because the material's weight will create a unique atmosphere in the room. Megan Pulson, founder and chief designer at 22 Design House, says that the location of the house, architectural style, and the intended use of the space are important factors to consider when combining patterns and textures.

Test it first

Tips for combining patterns and textures next is to conduct a trial first. Before starting to design a house, it's a good idea to determine the color palette first. Then, look for patterns that complement that color. By using Consistent colors make patterns appear more cohesive. Jenssy Williamson, the lead designer at West Rose Design, explains that you can conduct a test by taking a fabric sample and placing it next to another fabric. Then, observe the color combination, and you can judge whether the pattern and colors match or not.

Dress up with decorations made from leather, wood, and glass.

The material of hemp or velvet can indeed enhance the aesthetics of a home. However, this beauty is incomplete without decorations or furniture made from leather, wood, and glass.
